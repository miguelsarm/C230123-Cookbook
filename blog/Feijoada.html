Traditional Brazilian Feijoada

BY LEO

March 18, 2024

INGREDIENTS

  • 600 grams of dried beef, cut into sticks
  • 450 grams of salted pork ribs, cut into strips
  • 450 grams of salted pork loin, diced
  • 500 grams of fresh pork ribs, cut into strips
  • 400 grams of pork loin, diced
  • 1 kilogram of black beans
  • 400 grams of beef brisket, diced
  • 4 liters of water
  • 400 grams of Portuguese sausage, sliced
  • 500 grams of paio sausage, cut into 3 parts
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 300 grams of bacon, cut into sticks
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of minced garlic
  • 1 minced bird's eye chili (without seeds)
  • 2 bay leaves

COOKING INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a large pot with boiling water, add 600 grams of dried beef cut into sticks (8 centimeters by 2 centimeters), 450 grams of salted pork ribs cut into strips, 450 grams of salted pork loin diced, 500 grams of fresh pork ribs cut into strips, and 400 grams of pork loin diced. Boil for 20 minutes.
  2. Remove the meats from the heat, drain, and set aside.
  3. Place 1 kilogram of black beans in a pressure cooker over medium heat with 400 grams of beef brisket diced and 4 liters of water. Cover the pressure cooker and after it starts to pressurize, count 10 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and set aside.
  5. In the same pot where the meats boiled, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil and add 300 grams of bacon cut into sticks. Fry until golden brown.
  6. Add 1 large chopped onion, 1 tablespoon of minced garlic, and 1 minced bird's eye chili (without seeds), and saut√© until fragrant.
  7. Add 2 bay leaves, the reserved boiled meats, and the cooked beans with the beef brisket along with the broth. Mix well and cook over low heat for 1 hour and 20 minutes.
  8. Remove from heat and serve immediately with rice, farofa, collard greens, and pork cracklings. You can add orange slices to enhance the flavor.
